Age of Genesis

In the timeless expanse of the Age of Genesis, the world existed as a surreal realm of sublime formlessness—a cosmic canvas that had yet to be painted with the brushstrokes of defining forces. Archaic trees of the cosmos, colossal and ancient, stretched limitlessly into the void, their branches reaching towards unseen realms. The universe, in a state of serene equilibrium, reveled in the delicate dance of cosmic energies that transcended the comprehension of beings yet to come. Inhabiting this primordial landscape were mysterious and primal entities, embodiments of the very essence of existence. Formless and ethereal, these beings were the denizens of a reality that defied the conventional notions that would later shape the cosmos. In their patient vigil, these enigmatic beings were not bound by the linear passage of time but existed in a perpetual present, where the eons unfolded in a timeless dance. The Age of Genesis stood as the silent witness to the truest essence of the cosmos—a canvas unmarred by the forces seeking to impose their design, a testament to the enduring purity that lay at the heart of existence.

Age of Aether

With the Advent of Aether, the tranquil existence of the Age of Genesis was disrupted by an event of unparalleled significance. Aether, the fundamental force that introduced Disparity, swept through the cosmos, bringing with it the dualities of heat and cold, life and death, and light and dark. In response to this infusion of Aether, the Primordial 7 gods were born, each claiming dominion over specific aspects of Aether itself, meticulously guiding and controlling its vast potential. Stelarus, the God of Solar, harnessed the radiant energies of Aether, commanding the essence of fire to shape the celestial bodies that illuminated the cosmos. Raljun, the God of Arc, wove the threads of Aether into the tapestry of the arcane, manipulating the very essence of magic and mysticism. Eridus, the God of Void, took upon the solemn duty of controlling the dark currents of Aether. Nocturna, the Goddess of Death, guided the ebb and flow of Aether in realms beyond mortal comprehension, overseeing the transition from life to afterlife. Odin, the God of Conflict, utilized Aether to fuel the flames of strife and war, channeling its power into the struggles that defined the mortal plane. Hyperia, the Goddess of Time, wove the strands of Aether into the fabric of temporal existence, orchestrating the flow of moments within the cosmic tapestry. Vitalius, the God of Life, nurtured the fertile aspects of Aether, infusing vitality into the Arch-Trees and sowing the seeds of life across the mortal realm. While controlling the Aether the gods kept it from surging out of control. In their celestial realms, the gods crafted the mortal world, sowing the seeds of creation within the Arch-Trees. These majestic trees, conduits of Aether, served as vessels for the divine essence, spreading the life-giving force across the realms. The gods carefully maintained control over Aether, ensuring its balance and preventing the force from spiraling into chaotic discord. During this era, the dragons, blessed by the gods with a harmonious connection to Aether, ruled the lands with seemingly limitless power. The divine essence coursed through their veins, making them formidable beings. Mortals, guided and influenced by the gods, flourished in a world shaped by the delicate control of Aether, living in harmony with the divine forces that shaped their existence.

        During the lengthiest span of the Age of Aether, the dominion of the world lay in the formidable claws of dragons. Despite their existence in the solitude of sequestered lives, their supremacy proved insurmountable. However, the Eldarin and Moros, perceiving the dragons as a looming menace due to their unparalleled might, meticulously devised a strategy to initiate their campaign against the draconic rulers. Although the Draconians opposed this course of action, their resistance faltered in the face of the formidable military might wielded by the Eldarin and Moros. Post the eradication of dragons, a triumvirate of dominant species emerged as the new architects of the world – the Draconians, Eldarin, and Moros. Humanity found itself relegated to the status of a subservient species, lacking the celestial attributes that distinguished the three dominant races. In the eyes of the triumvirate, humans were deemed a slave race, shackled by their inferiority. Yet, in the shadow of oppression, a flicker of hope arose. The Golden Order, conceived by a determined cadre of humans, aimed to liberate humanity from its subjugated fate. The subsequent Aztrus Empire materialized as the embodiment of this noble cause, achieving the unification of humanity and severing the chains of millennia-old oppression. The Golden Order's pursuit of freedom culminated in the empowerment of humanity and the rise of an empire that echoed with the resounding victory over the dominion that once held them captive.

Age of Fragmentation

After the Shattering, the mortal realm plunged into the chaotic embrace of the Age of Fragmentation. The once-unified Pantheon of gods, who had meticulously woven the cosmic tapestry, now seemed to withdraw, their celestial presence becoming elusive and distant. Mortals, left to grapple with the aftermath of the cosmic upheaval, found themselves navigating a world reshaped by the fractures that echoed through the realms. The Arch-Trees, once harmonious conduits of divine power, experienced a profound transformation. Blooming like never before, these majestic entities absorbed the unbridled Aether that surged through the breach, branching into a riotous display of colors and energies. The mortal world, once a realm carefully crafted by the gods, now bore witness to the untamed forces of Aether, coursing through the Arch-Trees and permeating the very essence of existence. In this tumultuous era, the world began its gradual regression toward the formless state of the Age of Genesis. The shattered remnants of divine realms cast their influence upon the mortal plane, creating landscapes that bore both the scars of conflict and the promise of untapped potential. The boundaries between the divine and mortal blurred, as the lingering echoes of the Shattering resonated through every corner of the realms. As the shackles that once bound Aether were shattered, mortals found themselves able to absorb the essence of Aether at an unprecedented rate. The empowered became the most potent they had ever been, their very beings resonating with the unfettered power of Aether. Cities rose and fell, empires clashed, and newfound abilities manifested in individuals who now stood as living conduits of Aether.

        After the Shattering humans could finally start their stand against the Draconians, Eldarin, and Moros. The Shattering caused the humans, beings with a meager connection to Aether to be able to forge a more profound link with it. This eventually culminates in the eradication of the Draconians, Eldarin, and Moros, similar to the eradication of the dragons. The tumultuous events continue with the discovery and utilization of the World Stone, channeling the power to usher prosperity into the Aztrus Imperium. However, strife follows as Novrum falls to Zognalith, plunging the mortal realm into the chaos of a demon invasion. Amidst this turmoil, the Forsaken Emperor embarks on a crusade against Draconians and Eldarins, sparking the largest battle among the gifted. The Forsaken Emperor vanishes, leaving a fractured world and a power vacuum. As centuries unfold, vampires stealthily manipulate kingdoms from the shadows, and the Nashor Empire rises, facing conflict and eventual destruction. The Tsuki Ōkoku Empire emerges from the remnants of the shattered Moon Kingdom. Antares, a pivotal figure, births the Antarian Empire and battles vampires, leading to the rise of the Antarian Empire. The Age of Fracture continues with the mass murder of the arc-born, the ascent of a united Viking kingdom, and the Holy Antarian Empire's declaration of wars. The Golden Order, ARF (Antarian Resistance Front), and the Free Antarian Movement play significant roles in the unfolding drama. Julius, Zovjun, and a blood father rise, sparking conflicts that shape the landscape.

The shattering  

At the heart of the shattering stood Eridus, the enigmatic God of Void, his influence looming over the fate of the world. Eridus, harbinger of the power of void, made a fateful choice that would reverberate through the realms. Eridus, the most powerful of the Primordial 7 gods, felt another presence in the void, a presence previously unknown, intrigued he chose to defy his sacred duty as the god of void. In a moment of rebellion, he relinquished his grip on the shackles that restrained the raw power of Aether. The other gods, entrusted with maintaining the delicate balance of existence, could not anticipate the consequences of Eridus's bold decision. The very fabric of the world quivered as the ethereal shackles binding Aether shattered into a myriad of fractured fragments. The cosmic force, once harmoniously channeled and restrained by the gods, now roared unchecked, unrestrained by divine hands. Waves of chaotic energy emanated from the breach, echoing through the celestial realms and cascading into the mortal plane. Mortals, too, felt the aftershocks of The Shattering. The Aether, no longer restrained by divine guidance, manifested in unpredictable ways. As The Shattering unfolded, the delicate balance of existence crumbled. The gods, faced with the consequences of their brother's rebellion, withdrew from direct intervention, leaving the fractured realms in the hands of mortals now navigating a world on the brink of chaos.

World Changing Events

The Great Dragon Purge

An unprovoked assault that unfolded with the suddenness of a storm, catching the dragons off-guard in their isolated domains. The dragons, known for their individualistic and reclusive tendencies, struggled to organize a unified response. Their lack of communication hindered swift mobilization, and in those initial moments of chaos, the dragons faced the stark reality of their vulnerability. As the Eldarin and Moros forces pressed on, the dragons began to rally. However, time proved a ruthless adversary, slipping away as the dragons sought to form a united front. The invaders, relentless and seemingly without reason, continued their siege unabated. When the fate of dragonkind hung in the balance, Sirellius, the Golden Monarch, emerged as a beacon of unwavering courage. Alone, he faced the tidal wave of Eldarin and Moros forces, a solitary guardian against an overwhelming onslaught. The skies trembled as his golden scales blazed with a final, desperate brilliance. Sirellius, with a heart heavy with the burden of his species, became a living bulwark. Against insurmountable odds, he held the line, buying precious moments for the remaining dragons to escape the maelstrom of destruction. The battlefield echoed with the clash of elements, a symphony of devastation where the roar of dragons mingled with the battle cries of the aggressors.

Demigods

Demigods are beings of mystical origin, born of the union between a divine being and a mortal. Their existence lies at the crossroads of two worlds, and their very nature reflects this duality. The divine blood that flows through their veins imbues them with otherworldly powers, while their human ancestry grants them the ability to understand the struggles and joys of mortals. Like a bridge between the divine and the mortal, demigods are both revered and feared, as they possess the ability to reshape the very fabric of reality with their powers. Yet, despite their immense power, demigods are not invincible, and they too are subject to the same flaws and limitations as their mortal counterparts. It is this complex and enigmatic nature that has captivated the human imagination for centuries, and continues to inspire wonder and awe to this day. Throughout history, tales of demigods have captured the human imagination, and their legends have been passed down from generation to generation. However, despite their enduring presence in folklore and mythology, very little is known about the true nature of these beings. It is unclear if demigods ever truly existed, or if they are purely the stuff of legend. Even if they did exist, it is unknown if any demigods are still alive today, or if their bloodlines have been diluted to the point of obscurity. The mysteries surrounding demigods only add to their enigmatic and mystical nature, inspiring wonder and curiosity in those who seek to unravel the secrets of the divine and the mortal realms. Perhaps one day, more will be revealed about these half-human, half-divine beings, but until then, their legends will continue to endure as a testament to the power of myth and imagination.

Fearing it to be a trap orchestrated by the organization, Julius took his elite bodyguards along for protection. However, during the mission, an assassination attempt was made on Julius's life. Six skilled solar users, individuals with the ability to manipulate solar energy, launched a coordinated attack. However, in death, Julius would become an eternal slave to Trezzok.

Realizing that a direct confrontation with the secret order could prove disastrous, Julius opted for a more covert approach. He formed an underground movement aimed at garnering support for a new regime. By rallying like-minded individuals who shared his vision for change, Julius aimed to establish a solid foundation of supporters who would assist him when he ultimately rose to power. This calculated strategy ensured that the transition to his leadership would be smoother, minimizing the need for excessive correction once he assumed control.

He crafted a story about his bodyguards heroically saving him, pledging his unwavering loyalty to the Antarian Empire. This fabrication aimed to create confusion and buy him time, slowing down the next assassination attempt. United by a common enemy, the people blamed the secret order and the gifted individuals for their woes. Julius leveraged the long-lasting war and the devastating Battle of Primorium as examples of the chaos caused by the gifted and the secret organization. He painted a picture of a world where the corrupt and the wealthy profited from the suffering of the masses.

Moreover, the emperor was safeguarded by the Sons of Antares, an elite group of defenders. Killing them all in a single strike seemed impossible. Thus, Julius devised a long-term plan to eliminate the Sons of Antares one by one over the course of many years.

Sensing the shared goals and burning hatred within Zovjun, Julius recognized an opportunity for an alliance. He engaged Zovjun in discussions and proposed a deal: Zovjun would assist in the coup by eliminating the emperor and the Sons of Antares, and in return, Julius would provide crucial information. This included the location of the arc stone, the void stone, and details regarding Zovjun’s false father.

The partnership with Zovjun proved to be a crucial turning point in Julius's quest for power. Their shared determination and complementary skills offered a greater chance of success in toppling the emperor and dismantling the current regime. With this newfound alliance, Julius's plans shifted, moving away from a gradual approach to a more decisive and swift strike against their enemies. Together, they would reshape the empire and exact revenge on those who had perpetuated its corruption and oppression.

The plan for the coup was meticulously staged, with each step carefully calculated to maximize their chances of success. Zovjun, the formidable arc user, was intentionally captured and imprisoned in a facility near the Antarian capital. This prison was designed to suppress the powers of gifted individuals, but the suppression proved insufficient to hold back Zovjun's immense strength. Unbeknownst to the captors, the prison also housed the coveted void stone, a source of immense power.

Julius, working from behind the scenes, orchestrated a series of maneuvers to weaken the Sons of Antares, the formidable defenders of the emperor. He strategically dispatched as many of the Sons of Antares as possible, dispersing their forces and making the opposition within the palace more manageable for Zovjun.

While in his cell, Zovjun bided his time, waiting for the right moment to unleash his empowered self. As chaos unfolded within the capital, Julius utilized the chaos to his advantage. He led his elite private army down the Antarian road towards the palace, issuing orders to open fire on any gifted individuals they encountered. The ensuing pandemonium and violence created an atmosphere of unrest, further fueling the people's support for Julius and his movement.

As the chaos swirled around them, Zovjun tapped into the immense power within him, becoming increasingly empowered. His belief in his own unrivaled strength and the determination to defeat the oppressive regime drove him forward. With Julius's forces causing mayhem throughout the city, Zovjun finally broke free from his prison, unleashing devastation upon everything in his path.

Outside the throne room, Zovjun and Julius converged for the final battle. The clash between Zovjun and the remaining defenders of the empire was fierce and arduous. Both sides fought with unwavering determination, knowing that the outcome of this confrontation would determine the future of the Antarian Empire.

In the end, Zovjun and Julius emerged as the victors, Zovjun’s strength and Julius' strategic planning prevailing against the forces of the regime. Their hard-fought win solidified Julius' position as the new leader of the empire, heralding a new era of change and reform. The people, witnessing the downfall of the old regime were in disarray. The Mighty Empire had fallen.

After successfully seizing power and proclaiming himself the emperor of Antaria, Julius finds himself facing a fractured empire. The audacity of an outsider claiming the title of emperor, breaking away from the long-established Antarian dynasty, stirs deep anger and resentment among the people. The empire is torn apart by the division caused by Julius's rise to power.

In the wake of the coup, a power vacuum is created, and new nations emerge to fill the void. Two prominent factions arise: the Golden Order and the Free People of Antaria (FPA). These factions represent opposing ideals and vie for control over the splintered remnants of the empire.

Julius, now facing a daunting power struggle, recognizes the urgent need to reunite the broken empire and heal its wounds. However, his methods of achieving unity are ruthless and merciless. The first order of business for Julius is to plunder the wealth of the rich, freezing their market shares and seizing their assets. With a calculated and cold-hearted approach, he drains them of their resources.

But Julius's thirst for vengeance goes even further. Once he has extracted all their wealth, he orders the execution of every family suspected to be associated with the secret order. No mercy is shown, not even to innocent children. This brutal event becomes known as the Antarian Bloodbath, forever staining Julius's reign with bloodshed and cruelty.

The Antarian Bloodbath deepens the rift within the empire, as the atrocities committed by Julius further polarize the population. While some may view these extreme measures as necessary to eradicate corruption, others are repulsed by the ruthless manner in which justice is dispensed.

Julius faces the immense challenge of not only reunifying the empire but also reconciling the divided hearts and minds of the people. The scars of the Bloodbath run deep, and it will take more than force to heal the wounds inflicted upon Antaria.

        Characteristics: Solid Stone Composition: The Mrotog is primarily composed of almost pure rock, its entire form emanating an aura of hardened strength. Its exterior shell is rugged, displaying various textures, cracks, and weathered patterns, further enhancing its imposing appearance.

Mountainous Regions: The Mrotog feels at home in rugged, mountainous terrains. Caverns and Caves: Deep within underground caverns or within the depths of dark caves, the Mrotog could find solace during its dormant periods. These sheltered environments would shield it from the elements and provide a secluded space for rest and introspection.

Rock Formations and Cliffside Outcrops: The Mrotog would be drawn to rocky landscapes with intricate formations and cliffside outcrops. These areas would allow it to blend seamlessly with its surroundings and take advantage of its natural camouflage. Such habitats would provide ample hiding spots and protection during periods of inactivity.

Desolate Wastelands: Barren wastelands, devoid of significant vegetation and dominated by rocky terrain, would be suitable habitats for the Mrotog. These desolate areas would offer solitude and limited human interference, allowing the creature to remain undisturbed during its dormant phases.

Ruins and Ancient Structures: The Mrotog might also gravitate toward ancient ruins and structures, drawn to the remnants of civilizations long past. These sites would provide a sense of history and perhaps hold a connection to the Mrotog's origins. They could serve as resting grounds and places of contemplation for the creature.

Abilities: Strength of the Gods, the Mrotog is extremely strong for its stature and can easily lift hundreds of tons.
